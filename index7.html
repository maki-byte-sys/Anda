About Aguilar

Aguilar, Pangasinan is a coastal municipality with pristine beaches, lush hills, and rich culture. A perfect destination for relaxation and adventure, the town offers scenic waterfalls, panoramic viewpoints, and local culinary delights.

Why visit? Aguilar combines natural beauty, cultural heritage, and friendly communities. It's ideal for travelers seeking both adventure and serenity — from beach lounging, hiking, and waterfall treks to experiencing local festivals and cuisine.

Best time to visit: The dry season, from November to May, is ideal for exploring outdoor attractions, enjoying water activities, and attending local festivals without interruption from heavy rains.

History & Facts

Aguilar was officially founded in the early 19th century and is known for its fishing and agricultural heritage.

The town celebrates traditional festivals annually, including the Bangus Festival, highlighting local culture.

Its geography includes stunning coastlines, rolling hills, and hidden waterfalls.

How to Get There

By Car

Take Pangasinan road north from Dagupan City towards San Fabian, then follow the signs to Aguilar. Approx. 1h 20min.

By Bus / Van

Local buses and vans from Dagupan City head to Aguilar town proper. Approx. 1.5–2 hours.

Tips

Start early to enjoy the sights and avoid traffic. Bring cash for local transport.
